Attention Mechanism

ML Medium
Feynman Method
Explanation Date
1 Cold Explanation

Attention allows the model to figure out which parts of the input are most relevant when processing each token. Each position can look at all other positions and weight their contribution.

Accuracy: 72%

Good intuition, but missing the Q/K/V projection mechanism and the scaling factor that prevents gradient vanishing.

2 Research

Definition

A mechanism that computes context-sensitive representations by scoring query-key dot products, normalizing with softmax, then summing over values: Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QKᵀ/√d_k)V.

Do your research now, then come back to explain the concept again. Ask questions below as you study.

Why divide by √d_k?
Large d_k causes dot products to grow in magnitude, pushing softmax into regions with very small gradients. Scaling by √d_k counteracts this.
3 Refined Explanation

Attention projects input into queries Q, keys K, and values V. For each query, dot products with all keys give relevance scores, scaled by √d_k to keep gradients stable. Softmax turns scores into weights; the output is the weighted sum of V.

Accuracy: 95%

Excellent. Covers the full mechanism including scaling rationale.

Formatted Note

Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QKᵀ/√d_k) · V — projects into Q/K/V, scores pairwise relevance, scales to avoid vanishing gradients, normalizes via softmax, aggregates values.

Additional Notes

Multi-head attention runs h independent attention heads in parallel, then concatenates and projects — allowing the model to jointly attend to different representation subspaces.
